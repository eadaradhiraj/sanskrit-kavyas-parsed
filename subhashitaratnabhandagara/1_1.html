अथ स्वस्थाय देवाय नित्याय हतपाप्मने ।
त्यक्तक्रमविभागाय चैतन्यज्योतिषे नमः ॥ १-१॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
अथ अथ
Now / Here begins
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्) - Auspicious starting word
स्वस्थाय स्व-स्थाय
To the self-abiding / situated in Himself
Grammar Masculine, Dative, Singular
Samasa स्वे तिष्ठति इति स्वस्थः (उपपद-तत्पुरुषः), तस्मै
देवाय देवाय
To the Deity / God
Grammar Masculine, Dative, Singular (Base 'देव')
नित्याय नित्याय
To the eternal
Grammar Masculine, Dative, Singular (Base 'नित्य')
हतपाप्मने हत-पाप्मने
To the one whose sins are destroyed
Grammar Masculine, Dative, Singular (Base 'हतपाप्मन्'). 'हत' is Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः) from root हन्.
Samasa हतः पाप्मा यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः), तस्मै
त्यक्तक्रमविभागाय त्यक्त-क्रम-विभागाय
To the one devoid of sequence and division
Grammar Masculine, Dative, Singular. 'त्यक्त' is Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः) from root त्यज्.
Samasa क्रमस्य विभागः क्रमविभागः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), त्यक्तः क्रमविभागः येन सः (बहुव्रीहिः), तस्मै
चैतन्यज्योतिषे चैतन्य-ज्योतिषे
To the light of consciousness
Grammar Neuter (used in apposition to Devaya), Dative, Singular (Base 'ज्योतिस्')
Samasa चैतन्यम् एव ज्योतिः (कर्मधारयः), तस्मै
नमः नमः
Salutation / Bowing down
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्) - Governs the Dative case (चतुर्थी विभक्ति) for the recipient of the salutation.
Translation
"Now, salutations to the Deity who is self-abiding, eternal, whose sins are destroyed, who is devoid of sequence and division, and who is the light of pure consciousness."
