Shloka 361.5

अथ बद्धजटे रामे सुमन्त्रे गृहमागते ।
त्यक्तो राजा सुतत्यागादविश्वस्तैरिवासुभिः ॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
अथ अथ
Then / In this way
बद्धजटे बद्ध-जटे
When he had bound his hair into matted locks
Samasa बद्धाः जटाः येन सः, तस्मिन् (बहुव्रीहिः)
रामे रामे
When Rama (Locative Absolute)
Grammar Sati Saptami (रामे सति)
सुमन्त्रे सुमन्त्रे
When Sumantra (the charioteer)
गृहम् गृहम्
Home (Ayodhya)
आगते आगते
Arrived / Returned
त्यक्तः त्यक्तः
Was abandoned / Left
राजा राजा
The King (Dasharatha)
सुतत्यागात् सुत-त्यागात्
Due to separation/abandonment of the son
Samasa सुतस्य त्यागः, तस्मात् (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
अविश्वस्तैः अ-विश्वस्तैः
By untrustworthy / Faithless ones
Samasa न विश्वस्ताः, तैः (नञ्-तत्पुरुषः)
इव इव
Like / As if
असुभिः असुभिः
By life-breaths (Pranas)
Translation
"Then, when Rama had matted his hair (becoming an ascetic) and Sumantra had returned home alone, the King was abandoned by his life-breaths due to the separation from his son, just as one is abandoned by untrustworthy people."
