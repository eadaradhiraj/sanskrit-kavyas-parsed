गते मायावतंसे प्रातः पानमजागदीत् ।
हृदिस्थं दैवतं मूढ गवेषयसि किम् बहिः ॥ २ ॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
गते गते
Having gone / disappeared
Grammar Neuter, Locative, Singular. Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः) from root गम् (to go). Used in Locative Absolute.
मायावतंसे माया-अवतंसे
The illusory ornament (the false dawn)
Grammar Neuter, Locative, Singular (Used in Locative Absolute with 'गते')
Samasa माया एव अवतंसः (कर्मधारयः), तस्मिन्
प्रातः प्रातः
In the morning
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
पानम् पानम्
The tavern / The drink
Grammar Neuter, Nominative, Singular
अजागदीत् अजागदीत्
Cried out / Spoke
Grammar Aorist Tense (लुङ्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root गद् (to speak)
हृदिस्थम् हृदि-स्थम्
Abiding in the heart
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to दैवतम्)
Samasa हृदि तिष्ठति इति (उपपद-तत्पुरुषः)
दैवतम् दैवतम्
The deity
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular
मूढ मूढ
O fool!
Grammar Masculine, Vocative, Singular
गवेषयसि गवेषयसि
Do you search
Grammar Present Tense (लट्), Parasmaipada, 2nd Person, Singular - Root गवेष् (to seek / search)
किम् किम्
Why
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
बहिः बहिः
Outside
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
Translation
"When the illusory ornament (the phantom of false morning) had died away, the tavern cried out in the morning: 'O fool! Why do you search outside for the deity who abides within your heart?'"
