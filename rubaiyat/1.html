प्रबोधय प्रभातो नक्षत्राण्यन्तरदीधपत् ।
इनोऽक्षैप्सीन्नवीनांशुजालं राजगृहोपरि ॥ १ ॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
प्रबोधय प्रबोधय
Awake! / Wake up!
Grammar Imperative (लोट्), Parasmaipada, 2nd Person, Singular. Root बुध् (to wake) with prefix प्र and Causative (णिच्).
प्रभातः प्रभातः
The morning
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular (Base 'प्रभात'). Qualifies 'इनः' (The morning sun).
नक्षत्राणि नक्षत्राणि
The stars
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Plural
अन्तर् अन्तर्
Inside / Out of sight
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
अदीधपत् अदीधपत्
Made to disappear / Put to flight
Grammar Aorist Tense (लुङ्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root धा (to put) with Causative (णिच्).
इनः इनः
The sun / The lord
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular (Base 'इन')
अक्षैप्सीत् अक्षैप्सीत्
Flung / Threw
Grammar Aorist Tense (लुङ्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root क्षिप् (to throw).
नवीनांशुजालम् नवीन-अंशु-जालम्
The net of new rays
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular
Samasa नवीनाः अंशवः (कर्मधारयः), तेषां जालम् (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तत्
राजगृहोपरि राज-गृह-उपरि
Upon the king's palace
Grammar Indeclinable / Locative equivalent
Samasa राज्ञः गृहम् (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तस्य उपरि (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
Translation
"Awake! The morning sun has made the stars disappear (put the stars to flight), and has flung a net of new rays upon the king's palace (the Sultan's turret)."
