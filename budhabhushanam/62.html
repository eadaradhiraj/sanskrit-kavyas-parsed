यत्पादाः शिरसा न केन विधृताः पृथ्वीभृतां मध्यतः
तस्मिन्भास्वति राहुणा कवलिते लोकत्रयीचक्षुषि ।
खद्योतैः स्फुरितं तमोभिरुदितं ताराभिरुज्जुम्भितं
घूकैरुत्थितमत्र किं नु करवै किं केन नो चेष्टितम् ॥ ६२ ॥

Translation
"Whose rays (feet) were not borne on the head by whom among the mountains (or kings)? Yet, when that Sun, the very eye of the three worlds, is swallowed by Rahu (eclipsed)—fireflies start flashing, darkness rises up, stars shine forth, and owls become active. What can I say? Who does not act up (show their petty nature) when the great are in trouble?"
