स निर्विश्य यथाकामं तटेष्वालीनचन्दनौ ।
स्तनाविव दिशस्तस्याः शैलौ मलयदर्दुरौ ॥ ४-५१॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
स सः
He (Raghu)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Nominative, Singular
निर्विश्य निर्विश्य
Having enjoyed / Experienced
Grammar Indeclinable / Gerund (ल्यप्-प्रत्ययः) from root विश् with prefix निस् (निर्)
यथाकामम् यथा-कामम्
According to his desire / To his heart's content
Grammar Indeclinable Adverb
Samasa कामम् अनतिक्रम्य इति (अव्ययीभावः)
तटेषु तटेषु
On their slopes / banks
Grammar Neuter, Locative, Plural
आलीनचन्दनौ आलीन-चन्दनौ
Having sandalwood trees clinging to them
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Dual
Samasa आलीनाः चन्दनाः ययोः तौ (बहुव्रीहिः)
स्तनौ स्तनौ
Two breasts
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Dual
इव इव
Like / As if
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
दिशः दिशः
Of the direction
Grammar Feminine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'दिश्')
तस्याः तस्याः
Of her (the Southern direction)
Grammar Pronoun, Feminine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'तद्')
शैलौ शैलौ
The two mountains
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Dual (Base 'शैल')
मलयदर्दुरौ मलय-दर्दुरौ
Mount Malaya and Mount Dardura
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Dual
Samasa मलयश्च दर्दुरश्च (इतरेतर-द्वन्द्वः), तौ
Translation
"Having enjoyed to his heart's content the two mountains, Malaya and Dardura, which had sandalwood trees clinging to their slopes, he (Raghu) perceived them as if they were the two breasts of that Southern direction."
