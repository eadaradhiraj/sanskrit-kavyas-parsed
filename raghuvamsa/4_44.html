ततो वेलातटेनैव फलवत्पूगमालिना ।
अगस्त्याचरितामाशामनाशास्यजयो ययौ॥ ४-४४॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
ततः ततः
Then / After that
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
वेलातटेन वेला-तटेन
By the seashore
Grammar Neuter, Instrumental, Singular
Samasa वेलायाः तटम् (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तेन
एव एव
Only / Exactly
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
फलवत्पूगमालिना फलवत्-पूग-मालिना
Which was lined with rows of fruit-bearing areca-nut trees
Grammar Neuter, Instrumental, Singular (Adjective to वेलातटेन)
Samasa फलवन्तः ये पूगाः (कर्मधारयः), तेषां माला (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), सा अस्य अस्ति इति (इनि-प्रत्ययः), तेन
अगस्त्याचरिताम् अगस्त्य-आचरिताम्
Resorted to / treaded by Sage Agastya (the Southern direction)
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to आशाम्)
Samasa अगस्त्येन आचरिता (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः), ताम्
आशाम् आशाम्
The direction / Quarter
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular
अनाशास्यजयो अन्-आशास्य-जयः
He to whom victory came unsought (or whose victory was beyond hope for others)
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Samasa न आशास्यः अनाशास्यः (नञ्-तत्पुरुषः), अनाशास्यः जयः यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः)
ययौ ययौ
Went / Marched onwards
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root या (to go)
Translation
"Then, Raghu, to whom victory came unsought, marched onwards along the very seacoast—which was lined with rows of fruit-bearing areca-nut trees—towards the southern direction, which had once been treaded by the Sage Agastya."
