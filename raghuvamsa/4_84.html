कामरूपेश्वरस्तस्य हेमपीठाधिदेवताम् ।
रत्नपुष्पोपहारेण छायमानर्च पादयोः ॥ ४-८४॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
कामरूपेश्वरः कामरूप-ईश्वरः
The king of Kamarupa
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Samasa कामरूपाणाम् ईश्वरः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
तस्य तस्य
His (Raghu's)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Genitive, Singular
हेमपीठाधिदेवताम् हेम-पीठ-अधिदेवताम्
Which was like the presiding deity on the golden footstool
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to छायाम्)
Samasa हेम्नः पीठम् (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), हेमपीठस्य अधिदेवता (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), ताम्
रत्नपुष्पोपहारेण रत्न-पुष्प-उपहारेण
With the offering of gems acting as flowers
Grammar Masculine, Instrumental, Singular
Samasa रत्नानि एव पुष्पाणि (कर्मधारयः), तेषाम् उपहारः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तेन
छायाम् छायाम्
The splendor / The lustrous image
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular
आनर्च आनर्च
Worshipped / Adored
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root अर्च् (to worship) with prefix आ
पादयोः पादयोः
Of the two feet
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Dual
Translation
"The King of Kamarupa worshipped the splendid aura of Raghu's feet, which rested on a golden footstool as if it were its presiding deity, by offering precious gems as if they were flowers."
