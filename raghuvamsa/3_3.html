तदाननं मृत्सुरभि क्षितीश्वरो रहस्युपाघ्राय न तृप्तिमाययौ ।
करीव सिक्तं पृषतैः पयोमुचां शुचिव्यपाये वनराजिपल्वलम् ॥ ३-३॥

Translation
"Smelling her face in private—which was fragrant like the earth (due to her pregnancy craving for clay)—the Lord of the Earth (Dilipa) could not be satiated; just as an elephant is never satisfied smelling a forest pool sprinkled with rain-drops from the clouds at the end of the hot summer."
