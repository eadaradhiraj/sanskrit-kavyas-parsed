विशश्रमुर्नमेरूणां छायास्वध्यास्य सैनिकाः ।
दृषदो वासितोत्सङ्गा निषण्णमृगनाभिभिः ॥ ४-७४॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
विशश्रमुः विशश्रमुः
Rested / Relieved their fatigue
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Plural - Root श्रम् (to tire/rest) with prefix वि
नमेरूणाम् नमेरूणाम्
Of the Nameru trees
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Plural
छायासु छायासु
In the shades
Grammar Feminine, Locative, Plural
अध्यास्य अध्यास्य
Having occupied / Having sat upon
Grammar Indeclinable / Gerund (ल्यप्-प्रत्ययः) from root आस् (to sit) with prefix अधि
सैनिकाः सैनिकाः
The soldiers
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Plural
दृषदः दृषदः
The rocks / Stones
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Plural (Base 'दृषद्' - governed by अध्यास्य)
वासितोत्सङ्गाः वासित-उत्सङ्गाः
Whose surfaces were perfumed
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Plural (Adjective to दृषदः)
Samasa वासिताः उत्सङ्गाः यासां ताः (बहुव्रीहिः)
निषण्णमृगनाभिभिः निषण्ण-मृग-नाभिभिः
By the musk of the deer that had rested (there)
Grammar Masculine/Feminine, Instrumental, Plural
Samasa निषण्णाश्च ते मृगाश्च (कर्मधारयः), तेषां नाभयः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), ताभिः
Translation
"His soldiers rested in the cool shades of the Nameru trees, having seated themselves upon the rocks whose surfaces were perfumed by the musk of the deer that had previously rested there."
