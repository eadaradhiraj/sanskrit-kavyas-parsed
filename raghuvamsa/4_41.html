द्विषां विषह्य काकुत्स्थस्तत्र नाराचदुर्दिनम् ।
सन्मङ्गलस्नात इव प्रतिपेदे जयश्रियम्॥ ४-४१॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
द्विषाम् द्विषाम्
Of the enemies
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Plural (Base 'द्विष्')
विषह्य विषह्य
Having tolerated / Endured
Grammar Indeclinable / Gerund (ल्यप्-प्रत्ययः) from root सह् (to bear) with prefix वि
काकुत्स्थः काकुत्स्थः
The descendant of Kakutstha (Raghu)
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
तत्र तत्र
There (on Mt. Mahendra)
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
नाराचदुर्दिनम् नाराच-दुर्दिनम्
A shower of steel arrows
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular

As one who had taken an auspicious ceremonial bath
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Samasa सत् च तत् मङ्गलं च (कर्मधारयः), तस्मै स्नातः (चतुर्थी-तत्पुरुषः)

Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
प्रतिपेदे प्रतिपेदे
Gained / Obtained
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Atmanepada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root पद् with prefix प्रति
जयश्रियम् जय-श्रियम्
The Goddess of Victory
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular
Samasa जयस्य श्रीः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), ताम्
Translation
"There (on Mt. Mahendra), the descendant of Kakutstha (Raghu) patiently endured the shower of steel arrows from his enemies, and thus appearing as if he had taken the scripturally auspicious bath of consecration, he attained the Goddess of Victory."
