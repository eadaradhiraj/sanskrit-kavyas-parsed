Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
स सः
He (Raghu)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Nominative, Singular
सेनां सेनाम्
The army
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular
महतीं महतीम्
Huge / Great
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to सेनाम्)
कर्षन् कर्षन्
Drawing / Leading
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular. Present Active Participle (शतृ-प्रत्ययः) from root कृष् (to pull / draw).
पूर्वसागरगामिनीम् पूर्व-सागर-गामिनीम्
Marching towards the Eastern Ocean
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular
Samasa पूर्वः सागरः (कर्मधारयः), तं गच्छति इति (उपपद-तत्पुरुषः), ताम्
बभौ बभौ
Shone / Appeared
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root भा (to shine)
हरजटाभ्रष्टां हर-जटा-भ्रष्टाम्
Fallen from the matted locks of Lord Shiva (Hara)
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular. 'भ्रष्ट' is Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः).
Samasa हरस्य जटाः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), ताभ्यः भ्रष्टा (पञ्चमी-तत्पुरुषः), ताम्
गङ्गाम् गङ्गाम्
The river Ganga
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular
इव इव
Like
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
भगीरथः भगीरथः
King Bhagiratha
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Translation
"Leading his huge army marching towards the Eastern Ocean, he (Raghu) shone like King Bhagiratha leading the river Ganga that had fallen from the matted locks of Lord Shiva (Hara)."
