असह्यविक्रमः सह्यं दूरान्मुक्तमुदन्वता ।
नितम्बमिव मेदिन्याः स्रस्तांशुकमलङ्घयत् ॥ ४-५२॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
असह्यविक्रमः असह्य-विक्रमः
He of unbearable valor
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Samasa असह्यः विक्रमः यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः)
सह्यम् सह्यम्
The Sahya mountain
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Singular
दूरात् दूरात्
From afar / Left far behind
Grammar Adverbial Ablative, Singular (Base 'दूर')
मुक्तम् मुक्तम्
Abandoned / Left
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to सह्यम्). Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः) from root मुच् (to release / abandon).
उदन्वता उदन्वता
By the ocean
Grammar Masculine, Instrumental, Singular (Base 'उदन्वत्' - possessing water)
नितम्बम् नितम्बम्
The hip / The mountain slope
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Singular
इव इव
Like / As if
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
मेदिन्याः मेदिन्याः
Of the earth
Grammar Feminine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'मेदिनी')
स्रस्तांशुकम् स्रस्त-अंशुकम्
From which the garment has slipped
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to नितम्बम्)
Samasa स्रस्तम् अंशुकं यस्मात् सः (बहुव्रीहिः)
अलङ्घयत् अलङ्घयत्
Scaled / Crossed over
Grammar Imperfect Tense (लङ्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root लङ्घ् (to leap over / cross)
Translation
"He (Raghu), of unbearable valor, crossed the Sahya mountain, which had been left far behind by the receding ocean, making it look just like the hip of the Earth from which the garment (of waters) had slipped down."
