तस्योत्सृष्टनिवासेषु कण्ठरज्जुक्षतत्वचः ।
गजवर्ष्म किरातेभ्यः शशंसुर्देवदारवः॥ ४-७६॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
तस्य तस्य
His (Raghu's)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Genitive, Singular
उत्सृष्टनिवासेषु उत्सृष्ट-निवासेषु
At the abandoned campsites / halting places
Grammar Masculine, Locative, Plural
Samasa उत्सृष्टाः निवासाः यैः (बहुव्रीहिः) वा उत्सृष्टाश्च ते निवासाश्च (कर्मधारयः), तेषु
कण्ठरज्जुक्षतत्वचः कण्ठ-रज्जु-क्षत-त्वचः
Whose bark was bruised by the neck-ropes (of elephants)
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Plural (Adjective to देवदारवः)
Samasa कण्ठस्य रज्जवः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), ताभिः क्षताः (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः), कण्ठरज्जुक्षताः त्वचः येषां ते (बहुव्रीहिः)
गजवर्ष्म गज-वर्ष्म
The height / proportion of the elephants
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Base 'वर्ष्मन्')
Samasa गजानां वर्ष्म (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तत्
किरातेभ्यः किरातेभ्यः
To the tribal hunters / Kiratas
Grammar Masculine, Dative, Plural
शशंसुः शशंसुः
Indicated / Told
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Plural - Root शंस् (to praise / indicate)
देवदारवः देव-दारवः
The Deodar trees
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Plural (Base 'दारु')
Samasa देवानां दारवः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
Translation
"At the halting places left behind by his army, the Deodar trees, whose bark was bruised by the neck-ropes of his elephants, indicated the immense height of those elephants to the tribal hunters (Kiratas)."
