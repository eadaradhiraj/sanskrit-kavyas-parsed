वङ्गानुत्खाय तरसा नेता नौसाधनोद्यतान् ।
निचखान जयस्तम्भान्गङ्गास्रोतोन्तरेषु सः॥ ४-३६॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
वङ्गान् वङ्गान्
The Vangas (people of Bengal)
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Plural
उत्खाय उत्खाय
Having uprooted / Defeated completely
Grammar Indeclinable / Gerund (ल्यप्-प्रत्ययः) from root खन् with prefix उद्
तरसा तरसा
With force / With speed
Grammar Neuter, Instrumental, Singular (Base 'तरस्')
नेता नेता
The leader (Raghu)
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular (Base 'नेतृ')
नौसाधनोद्यतान् नौ-साधन-उद्यतान्
Those who were ready with their naval forces
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Plural
Samasa नौः एव साधनम् (कर्मधारयः), तेन उद्यताः (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः), तान्
निचखान निचखान
Dug in / Planted
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root खन् with prefix नि
जयस्तम्भान् जय-स्तम्भान्
The pillars of victory
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Plural
Samasa जयस्य स्तम्भान् (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
गङ्गास्रोतोन्तरेषु गङ्गा-स्रोतः-अन्तरेषु
In the lands/islands between the streams of the Ganga
Grammar Neuter, Locative, Plural
Samasa गङ्गायाः स्रोतांसि (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तेषाम् अन्तराणि (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तेषु
सः सः
He
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Translation
"Having forcefully uprooted the Vangas, who were prepared for battle with their naval forces, that great leader (Raghu) firmly planted his pillars of victory in the islands situated between the streams of the river Ganga."
