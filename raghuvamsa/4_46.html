बलैरध्युषितास्तस्य विजिगीषोर्गतध्वनः ।
मारीचोद्भ्रान्तहारीता मलयाद्रेरुपत्यकाः॥ ४-४६॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
बलैर् बलैः
By the armies
Grammar Neuter, Instrumental, Plural
अध्युषितास् अध्युषिताः
Were occupied / Halting grounds
Grammar Feminine, Nominative, Plural (Adjective to उपत्यकाः). Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः) from root वस् (to dwell) with prefix अधि.
तस्य तस्य
His (Raghu's)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Genitive, Singular
विजिगीषोर् विजिगीषोः
Of the one eager to conquer
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular. Desiderative Noun (सन् + उ-प्रत्ययः) from root जि (to conquer) with prefix वि.
गतध्वनः गत-अध्वनः
Of the one who had traversed a long path
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'गताध्वन्')
Samasa गतः अध्वा येन सः (बहुव्रीहिः), तस्य
मारीचोद्भ्रान्तहारीता मारीच-उद्भ्रान्त-हारीताः
Where Harita birds fluttered up from the pepper groves
Grammar Feminine, Nominative, Plural (Adjective to उपत्यकाः)
Samasa मारीचेषु उद्भ्रान्ताः हारीताः यासु ताः (बहुव्रीहिः)
मलयाद्रेर् मलय-अद्रेः
Of the Malaya mountain
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular
Samasa मलयः एव अद्रिः (कर्मधारयः), तस्य
उपत्यकाः उपत्यकाः
The lands at the foot of a mountain / The lower slopes
Grammar Feminine, Nominative, Plural
Translation
"The lands at the foot of the Malaya mountain—where flocks of Harita birds fluttered up from the groves of black pepper—were occupied as halting grounds by the armies of that victory-seeking king (Raghu), who had traversed a long distance."
