Canto 4, Shloka 21

प्रससादोदयादम्भः कुम्भयोनेर्महौजसः ।
रघोरभिभवाशङ्कि चुक्षुभे द्विषतां मनः॥ ४-२१॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
प्रससाद प्रससाद
Became clear / calm
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root सद् (to sit/settle) with prefix प्र
उदयात् उदयात्
From the rise
Grammar Masculine, Ablative, Singular
अम्भः अम्भः
The water
Grammar Neuter, Nominative, Singular (Base 'अम्भस्')
कुम्भयोनेः कुम्भ-योनेः
Of the pitcher-born sage (Agastya)
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular
Samasa कुम्भः योनिः यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः)
महौजसः महा-ओजसः
Of the one with great power
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular
Samasa महत् ओजः यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः)
रघोः रघोः
From Raghu
Grammar Masculine, Ablative/Genitive, Singular
अभिभवाशङ्कि अभिभव-आशङ्कि
Fearing defeat
Grammar Neuter, Nominative, Singular (Base 'आशङ्किन्')
Samasa रघोः अभिभवः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तम् आशङ्कते इति (उपपद-तत्पुरुषः)
चुक्षुभे चुक्षुभे
Became agitated
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Atmanepada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root क्षुभ् (to be agitated)
द्विषताम् द्विषताम्
Of the enemies
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Plural. Present Active Participle (शतृ-प्रत्ययः) from root द्विष् (to hate)
मनः मनः
The mind
Grammar Neuter, Nominative, Singular (Base 'मनस्')
Translation
"The waters became clear due to the rise of the highly powerful sage Agastya (born from a pitcher); while the minds of the enemies, fearing defeat from Raghu, became intensely agitated."
