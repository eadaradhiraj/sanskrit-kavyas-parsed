Canto 5, Shloka 13

सर्वत्र नो वार्तमवेहि राजन्नाथे कुतस्त्वय्यशुभं प्रजानाम् ।
सूर्ये तपत्यावरणाय दृष्टेः कल्पेत लोकस्य कथं तमिस्रा ॥ ५-१३
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
सर्वत्र सर्वत्र
Everywhere / In every respect
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
नो नः
Our / To us
Grammar Pronoun, Dative/Genitive, Plural (Base 'अस्मद्')
वार्तमवेहि वार्तम् अवेहि
Know the well-being
Grammar वार्तम्: Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Base 'वार्त')
अवेहि: Root 'इ' (i) with prefix 'अव' (ava), Parasmaipada, Lot Lakara (Imperative), Madhyama Purusha (2nd Person), Ekavacana (Singular)
राजन्नाथे राजन् नाथे
O King, when the protector
Grammar राजन्: Masculine, Vocative, Singular (Base 'राजन्')
नाथे: Masculine, Locative, Singular (Base 'नाथ'). Used as Locative Absolute (सति सप्तमी).
कुतस्त्वय्यशुभं कुतः त्वयि अ-शुभम्
Wherefrom (can there be) inauspiciousness / ill in you
Grammar कुतः: Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
त्वयि: Pronoun, Locative, Singular (Base 'युष्मद्')
अ-शुभम्: Neuter, Nominative, Singular
Samasa न शुभम् इति अ-शुभम् (नञ्-तत्पुरुषः)
प्रजानाम् प्रजानाम्
For the subjects / people
Grammar Feminine, Genitive, Plural (Base 'प्रजा')
सूर्ये सूर्ये
When the sun
Grammar Masculine, Locative, Singular (Base 'सूर्य'). Locative Absolute (सति सप्तमी).
तपत्यावरणाय तपति आवरणाय
Is shining, for the obstruction
Grammar तपति: Present Participle (Shatr), Masculine, Locative, Singular (Base 'तपत्', from root 'तप्'). Locative Absolute.
आवरणाय: Neuter, Dative, Singular (Base 'आवरण')
दृष्टेः दृष्टेः
Of the eyesight
Grammar Feminine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'दृष्टि')
कल्पेत कल्पेत
Can bring about / Be capable
Grammar Root 'कॢप्' (kḷp), Atmanepada, Vidhilin Lakara (Potential/Optative Mood), Prathama Purusha (3rd Person), Ekavacana (Singular)
लोकस्य लोकस्य
Of the people / mankind
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'लोक')
कथं कथम्
How
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
तमिस्रा तमिस्रा
Pitch darkness
Grammar Feminine, Nominative, Singular (Base 'तमिस्रा')
Translation
"O King! Know that it is well with us in every respect. Whence can there be inauspiciousness (ill) for the subjects while you are their protector? How can pitch darkness bring about an obstruction to the eyesight of mankind while the sun is brightly shining?"
