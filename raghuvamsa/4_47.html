ससञ्जुरश्वक्षुण्णानामेलानामुत्पतिष्णवः ।
तुल्यगन्धिषु मत्तेभकटेषु फलरेणवः॥ ४-४७॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
ससञ्जुर् ससञ्जुः
Stuck / Adhered
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Plural - Root सञ्ज् (to stick / adhere)
अश्वक्षुण्णानाम् अश्व-क्षुण्णानाम्
Pounded by the horses (hooves)
Grammar Feminine, Genitive, Plural. 'क्षुण्ण' is Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः) from root क्षुद्.
Samasa अश्वैः क्षुण्णाः (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः), तासाम्
एलानाम् एलानाम्
Of the cardamoms
Grammar Feminine, Genitive, Plural
उत्पतिष्णवः उत्पतिष्णवः
Flying up / Naturally rising
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Plural (Adjective to फलरेणवः). Formed with the habit-denoting suffix (इष्णुच्-प्रत्ययः) on root पत् with prefix उद्.
तुल्यगन्धिषु तुल्य-गन्धिषु
Having a similar fragrance
Grammar Masculine, Locative, Plural
Samasa तुल्यः गन्धः येषां ते (बहुव्रीहिः), तेषु
मत्तेभकटेषु मत्त-इभ-कटेषु
On the temples of the infuriated elephants
Grammar Masculine, Locative, Plural
Samasa मत्ताश्च ते इभाः (कर्मधारयः), तेषां कटाः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तेषु
फलरेणवः फल-रेणवः
The dust particles of the fruits/seeds
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Plural
Samasa फलानां रेणवः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
Translation
"The fragrant dust particles of the cardamom seeds, being pounded by the hooves of the galloping horses, flew up into the air and stuck to the sweating temples of the infuriated elephants, which possessed a very similar fragrance."
