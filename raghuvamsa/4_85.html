Canto 4, Shloka 85

इति जित्वा दिशो जिष्णुर्न्यवर्तत रथोद्धतम् ।
रजो विश्रामयन्राज्ञां छत्रशून्येषु मौलिषु ॥ ४-८५॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
इति इति
Thus / In this manner
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
जित्वा जित्वा
Having conquered
Grammar Indeclinable / Gerund (क्त्वा-प्रत्ययः) from root जि
दिशो दिशः
The quarters / Directions
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Plural
जिष्णुर् जिष्णुः
The victorious one (Raghu)
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular (Base 'जिष्णु' - from root जि with इष्णुच्-प्रत्ययः)

Grammar Imperfect Tense (लङ्), Atmanepada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root वृत् with prefix नि

Raised by his chariot
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to रजः)
Samasa रथेन उद्धतम् (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः)

The dust
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Base 'रजस्')
विश्रामयन् विश्रामयन्
Causing to rest / Settling
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular. Present Active Participle (शतृ-प्रत्ययः) from root श्रम् with prefix वि and Causative (णिच्).

Of the kings
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Plural
छत्रशून्येषु छत्र-शून्येषु
Devoid of parasols (royal umbrellas)
Grammar Masculine, Locative, Plural (Adjective to मौलिषु)
Samasa छत्रेण शून्याः (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः), तेषु
मौलिषु मौलिषु
On the crowns / heads
Grammar Masculine, Locative, Plural
Translation
"Thus having conquered all the quarters, the ever-victorious Raghu returned to his kingdom, causing the dust raised by his chariot wheels to settle upon the heads of the defeated kings, which were now completely devoid of their royal parasols."
