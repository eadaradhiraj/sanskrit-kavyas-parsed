इक्षुच्छायनिषादिन्यस्तस्य गोप्तुर्गुणोदयम् ।
आकुमारकथोद्धातं शालिगोप्यो जगुर्यशः ॥ ४-२०॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
इक्षुच्छायनिषादिन्यः इक्षु-च्छाय-निषादिन्यः
Sitting in the shade of sugarcane plants
Grammar Feminine, Nominative, Plural (Base 'निषादिनी')
Samasa इक्षूणां छाया (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तस्यां निषीदन्ति इति (उपपद-तत्पुरुषः)
तस्य तस्य
His (Raghu's)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'तद्')
गोप्तुः गोप्तुः
Of the protector / King
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'गोप्तृ')
गुणोदयम् गुण-उदयम्
Marked by the manifestation of virtues
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to यशः)
Samasa गुणानाम् उदयः यस्मिन् तत् (बहुव्रीहिः)
आकुमारकथोद्धातम् आकुमार-कथा-उद्धातम्
The narration of which was begun from Raghu's childhood
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to यशः)
Samasa आकुमारं याः कथाः तासाम् उद्धातः (उपक्रमः) यस्मिन् तत् (बहुव्रीहिः)
शालिगोप्यः शालि-गोप्यः
The female guards of the shali fields
Grammar Feminine, Nominative, Plural (Base 'गोपी')
Samasa शालिनां गोप्यः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
जगुः जगुः
Sang
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Plural - Root गै (to sing)
यशः यशः
Fame / Glory
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Base 'यशस्')
Translation
"Sitting in the shade of the sugarcane plants, the female watchers of the autumn rice fields sang the glory of that protector (King Raghu)—a glory which was marked by the manifestation of his virtues and the stories of which were enthusiastically begun even by young children."
