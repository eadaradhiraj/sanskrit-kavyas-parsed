स ययौ प्रथमं प्राचीं तुल्यः प्राचीनबर्हिषा ।
अहिताननिलोद्धूतैस्तर्जयन्निव केतुभिः॥ ४-२८॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
स सः
He (Raghu)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Nominative, Singular (Base 'तद्')
ययौ ययौ
Went
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root या (to go)
प्रथमं प्रथमम्
First / At first
Grammar Indeclinable / Adverb (Accusative Singular)
प्राचीं प्राचीम्
To the Eastern direction
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular (Base 'प्राची')
तुल्यः तुल्यः
Equal to / Comparable to
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
प्राचीनबर्हिषा प्राचीन-बर्हिषा
Indra (who has sacred kusha grass placed eastward)
Grammar Masculine, Instrumental, Singular
Samasa प्राचीनानि बर्हींषि यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः) - An epithet of Indra.
अहितान् अहितान्
The enemies
Grammar Masculine, Accusative, Plural
Samasa न हिताः अहिताः (नञ्-तत्पुरुषः), तान्
अनिलोद्धूतैः अनिल-उद्धूतैः
Tossed by the wind
Grammar Masculine, Instrumental, Plural. 'उद्धूत' is Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः) from root धू with prefix उद्.
Samasa अनिलेन उद्धूताः (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः), तैः
तर्जयन् तर्जयन्
Threatening
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular. Present Active Participle (शतृ-प्रत्ययः) from root तर्ज् (to threaten).
इव इव
As if
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
केतुभिः केतुभिः
With banners / flags
Grammar Masculine, Instrumental, Plural (Base 'केतु')
Translation
"He, equal to Indra (Prachinabarhis), marched towards the East first, as if threatening the enemies with his banners that were tossed by the wind."
