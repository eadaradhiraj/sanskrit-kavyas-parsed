स गुप्तमूलप्रत्यन्तः शुद्धपार्ष्णिरयान्वितः ।
षड्विधं बलमादाय प्रतस्थे दिग्जिगीषया॥ ४-२६॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
स सः
He (Raghu)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Nominative, Singular (Base 'तद्')
गुप्तमूलप्रत्यन्तः गुप्त-मूल-प्रत्यन्तः
Whose base (capital) and frontiers were protected
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Samasa मूलं च प्रत्यन्ताश्च (द्वन्द्वः), गुप्ताः मूलप्रत्यन्ताः यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः)
शुद्धपार्ष्णिर् शुद्ध-पार्ष्णिः
Whose rear was secure / clear (of enemies)
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Samasa शुद्धा पार्ष्णिः यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः)
अयान्वितः अय-अन्वितः
Endowed with good fortune / auspicious rites
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular. अन्वित: Past Passive Participle (क्त-प्रत्ययः) from root इ with prefix अनु.
Samasa अयेन अन्वितः (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः)
षड्विधं षट्-विधम्
Six-fold
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Adjective to बलम्)
Samasa षट् विधाः यस्य तत् (बहुव्रीहिः)
बलम् बलम्
The army
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular
आदाय आदाय
Having taken
Grammar Indeclinable (ल्यप्-प्रत्ययः) from root दा (to give/take) with prefix आ
प्रतस्थे प्रतस्थे
Set out / Marched
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Atmanepada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root स्था (to stand) with prefix प्र
दिग्जिगीषया दिश्-जिगीषया
With the desire to conquer the quarters
Grammar Feminine, Instrumental, Singular. जिगीषा is formed with Desiderative (सन्-प्रत्ययः) from root जि (to conquer).
Samasa दिशां जिगीषा (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तया
Translation
"Having his capital and frontiers well-protected, and his rear secure, he (Raghu), endowed with good fortune, took his six-fold army and set out with the desire to conquer the quarters."
