स तीर्त्वा कपिशां सैन्यैर्बद्धद्विरदसेतुभिः ।
उत्कलादर्शितपथः कलिङ्गाभिमुखो ययौ॥ ४-३८॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
स सः
He (Raghu)
Grammar Pronoun, Masculine, Nominative, Singular
तीर्त्वा तीर्त्वा
Having crossed
Grammar Indeclinable / Gerund (क्त्वा-प्रत्ययः) from root तॄ (to cross)
कपिशाम् कपिशाम्
The river Kapisha
Grammar Feminine, Accusative, Singular
सैन्यैः सैन्यैः
With the armies
Grammar Neuter, Instrumental, Plural
बद्धद्विरदसेतुभिः बद्ध-द्विरद-सेतुभिः
Which formed a bridge of elephants
Grammar Neuter, Instrumental, Plural (Adjective to सैन्यैः)
Samasa द्विरदा एव सेतवः (कर्मधारयः), बद्धाः द्विरदसेतवः यैः (बहुव्रीहिः), तैः
उत्कलादर्शितपथः उत्कल-आदर्शित-पथः
Whose route was shown by the Utkalas
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Samasa उत्कलैः आदर्शितः (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः), उत्कलादर्शितः पथः यस्य सः (बहुव्रीहिः)
कलिङ्गाभिमुखो कलिङ्ग-अभिमुखः
Facing / Heading towards Kalinga
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Singular
Samasa कलिङ्गानाम् अभिमुखः (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
ययौ ययौ
Went / Marched
Grammar Perfect Tense (लिट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Singular - Root या (to go)
Translation
"Having crossed the river Kapisha with his armies that had formed a bridge using their elephants, he (Raghu) marched towards the province of Kalinga, taking the route shown by the kings of Utkala."
