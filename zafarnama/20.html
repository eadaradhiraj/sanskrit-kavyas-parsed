Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis

In the battlefield
Grammar Neuter, Locative, Singular
Samasa रणस्य क्षेत्रम् इति रण-क्षेत्रम् (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः), तस्मिन् ।

And

सेने सेने
Armies

भवेतामुपसज्जिते भवेताम् उपसज्जिते
May they be arrayed / prepared
Grammar भवेताम्: Root 'भू', Vidhilin (Potential), Prathama Purusha, Dual
उपसज्जिते: Past Passive Participle (Kta) of root 'सज्ज्' with 'उप', Feminine, Nominative, Dual
दूरादपि दूरात् अपि
Even from afar / from a distance
Grammar दूरात्: Ablative Singular of 'दूर' (used adverbially)
अपि: Indeclinable (Emphasis)
च च
And
Grammar Indeclinable
ये ये
Which (two armies)
Grammar Pronoun (Base 'यद्'), Feminine, Nominative, Dual (matching 'सेने')
द्रष्टुं द्रष्टुम्
To be seen / visible
Grammar तुमुन्-प्रत्ययः (Infinitive) of root 'दृश्' (to see)
भवेतामनिगूहिते भवेताम् अ-निगूहिते
May they be unconcealed / conspicuous
Grammar भवेताम्: Root 'भू', Vidhilin (Potential), Dual
अ-निगूहिते: Feminine, Nominative, Dual (Base 'अ-निगूहित')
Samasa न निगूहिते इति अनिगूहिते (नञ्-तत्पुरुषः)
Translation
Hindi: (अथवा) लड़ाई के मैदान में (मेरी और तुम्हारी) दोनों सेनाएँ पंक्तिबद्ध (तैनात) हों और दूर से ही बिना छिपे एक दूसरे को दिखाई पड़ती रहें।

English: (Or else), let both of our armies be arrayed in the field of battle, in such a way that they are clearly visible (unconcealed) from afar.
