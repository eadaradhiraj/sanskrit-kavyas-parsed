फलं त्वया सदा भुक्तमश्रान्तेन परार्जितम् ।
युयुत्सुभ्यो युवभ्यस्त्वं नादः समरजं फलम् ॥ २३ ॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
फलं फलम्
Fruit / reward
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Base 'फल')
त्वया त्वया
By you
Grammar Pronoun (Base 'युष्मद्'), Instrumental, Singular
सदा सदा
Always
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
भक्तमश्रान्तेन भुक्तम् अश्रान्तेन
Eaten/enjoyed, by one who is untired (without making effort)
Grammar भुक्तम्: Past Passive Participle (Kta) of root 'भुज्' (to eat/enjoy), Neuter, Accusative, Singular
अश्रान्तेन: Masculine, Instrumental, Singular (Base 'अश्रान्त')
Samasa न श्रान्तः इति अश्रान्तः (नञ्-तत्पुरुषः), तेन
परार्जितम् पर-अर्जितम्
Acquired by others
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular
Samasa परैः अर्जितम् इति परार्जितम् (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः)
(Note: Printed as 'पराज्जितम्' as a variant/scribal style)
युयुत्सुभ्यो युयुत्सुभ्यः
From those desiring to fight (warriors)
Grammar Masculine, Ablative, Plural (Base 'युयुत्सु', desiderative adjective from root 'युध')
युवभ्यस्त्वं युवभ्यः त्वम्
From the young (warriors), you
Grammar युवभ्यः: Masculine, Ablative, Plural (Base 'युवन्')
त्वम्: Pronoun, Nominative, Singular (Base 'युष्मद्')
नादः न आदः
Did not eat / taste
Grammar न: Indeclinable (Negative Particle)
आदः: Root 'अद्' (to eat), Lang Lakara (Imperfect Tense), 2nd Person, Singular
समरजं समर-जम्
Born of battle / combat
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Adjective qualifying 'फलम्')
Samasa समरे जायते इति समर-जम् (उपपद-तत्पुरुषः)
फलम् फलम्
Fruit / outcome
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular (Base 'फल')
Translation
Hindi: तूने हमेशा लाड़-चाव से (बिना मेहनत किये, बिना कष्ट उठाए) दूसरों के परिश्रम से अर्जित फलों (साम्राज्य का ऐश्वर्य) को खाया है। तूने कभी भी रणभूमि में लड़ने के इच्छुक युवा योद्धाओं का मुकाबला करके युद्ध से जनित फल (साहसिक विजय का आनंद) नहीं चखा है।

English: Thou hast always tasted the fruits of a pampered life acquired by the labor of others without making any effort thy self; never hast thou confronted the young warriors desirous of battle to taste the true fruits born of war.
