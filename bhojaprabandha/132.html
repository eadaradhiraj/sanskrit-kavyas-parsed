अदातृमानसं क्वापि न स्पृशन्ति कवेर्गिरः ।
दुःखायैवातिवृद्धस्य विलासास्तरुणीकृताः ॥ १३२॥
Padaccheda & Samasa Analysis
अदातृमानसम् अदातृ-मानसम्
The mind of a non-giver (miser)
Grammar Neuter, Accusative, Singular
Samasa न दाता इति अदाता (नञ्-तत्पुरुषः), अदातुः मानसम् (षष्ठी-तत्पुरुषः)
क्व क्व
Where
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
अपि अपि
Even (Together with 'क्व', it means 'Anywhere')
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
न न
Not
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
स्पृशन्ति स्पृशन्ति
Touch / Affect
Grammar Present Tense (लट्), Parasmaipada, 3rd Person, Plural - Root स्पृश् (to touch)
कवेः कवेः
Of the poet
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular (Base 'कवि')
गिरः गिरः
The words / The poems
Grammar Feminine, Nominative, Plural (Base 'गिर्')
दुःखाय दुःखाय
For distress / For sorrow
Grammar Neuter, Dative, Singular
एव एव
Only / Indeed
Grammar Indeclinable (अव्ययम्)
अतिवृद्धस्य अति-वृद्धस्य
Of an extremely old man
Grammar Masculine, Genitive, Singular
Samasa अतीव वृद्धः (प्रादि-समासः / कर्मधारयः), तस्य
विलासाः विलासाः
The amorous sports / Playful actions
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Plural
तरुणीकृताः तरुणी-कृताः
Performed by a young woman
Grammar Masculine, Nominative, Plural (Adjective to विलासाः)
Samasa तरुण्या कृताः (तृतीया-तत्पुरुषः)
Translation
"The words of a poet can never touch the mind of a non-giver (a miser who belittles their value). Similarly, the amorous sports performed by a young woman only lead to the distress of an extremely old man (due to his lack of vitality)."
